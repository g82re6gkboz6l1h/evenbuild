2. **Targeted Test Selection**: Focus on the specific tests that impact the software most, eliminating redundancy.
3. **Anomaly Detection**: Pinpoints irregularities in system behavior, addressing potential issues before they scale.

Addressing AI-Powered QA Challenges

Integrating AI into QA isn’t without hurdles, but forward-looking strategies can ease the transition. Common challenges include:

- **Budget Constraints**: High initial investment costs can deter smaller companies.
- **Skills Gap**: QA teams require training in AI and ML applications.
- **Data Quality Issues**: Low-quality historical data can affect AI model accuracy.
- **Resistance to Change**: Team members might hesitate to adopt AI-driven solutions.

### Possible Solutions:

- Use budget-friendly, customizable tools like [Zof AI](https://zof.ai) for gradual implementation.
- Introduce training programs for upskilling QA teams.
- Generate synthetic yet accurate datasets to train ML models.
- Drive cultural change to promote AI adoption across departments.
- Employ hybrid methodologies that blend traditional and AI-driven QA approaches.

The evolution of AI-driven QA highlights the need for both organizational readiness and technological advancements.
