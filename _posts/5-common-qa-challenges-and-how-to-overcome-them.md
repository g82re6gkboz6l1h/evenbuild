## 1. Common QA Challenges
Below are five frequent hurdles QA teams face:

### (a) **Insufficient Time for Thorough Testing**
Tight development cycles in Agile or DevOps workflows often pressurize QA teams to rush processes, increasing the likelihood of defects slipping into production.

### (b) **Unclear or Changing Requirements**
Ill-defined or constantly evolving project requirements create delays as test cases are redrafted to match updates.

### (c) **Limited Resources**
Inadequate personnel, tools, or infrastructure restrict QA teams' ability to meet modern testing demands.

### (d) **Challenges with Test Automation**
Many QA teams struggle with implementing automation due to skill gaps or lack of suitable tools.

### (e) **Managing Big Data and Test Complexities**
Creating and overseeing multifaceted testing environments is often tedious and error-prone.

## 2. The Impact on Software Development
QA obstacles can cause significant issues like: 

- **Project Delays**: Unresolved defects delay launches.
- **Higher Costs**: Late-stage fixes are more expensive.
- **Subpar Quality**: Rushed workflows compromise end-user satisfaction.
- **Team Burnout**: Insufficient tools and frequent issue handling exhaust staff morale.

Tackling these challenges promptly can protect timelines, budgets, and overall product quality.
