Automated QA testing uses tools and scripts to execute repetitive tasks faster and more accurately. This evolution is fuelled by:

- **DevOps and CI/CD Integration**: Automation aligns seamlessly with continuous integration and delivery, enhancing workflow efficiency.

Manual vs. Automated Testing: Which Is Better?

Each testing method has its place. Here’s a quick comparison:

### Manual Testing
- Involves human testers for exploratory or user-focused testing like UI/UX.
- Best for ad-hoc scenarios requiring creativity and intuition.
- Time-intensive with higher risk of human errors.
- Less scalable for large-scale projects.

### Automated Testing
- Runs pre-scripted tests using software, ideal for repetitive and performance-critical tasks.
- Scales well for extensive regression, load, or compatibility testing.
- Requires initial setup but offers significant long-term cost savings.
- Perfect for Agile and DevOps workflows due to its speed and accuracy.

In conclusion, manual testing is irreplaceable in creative contexts, but automation is the future for scalable, repetitive tasks.
