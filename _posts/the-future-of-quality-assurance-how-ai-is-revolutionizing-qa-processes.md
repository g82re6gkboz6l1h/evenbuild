## Common Challenges in Traditional QA
Traditional QA struggles to keep up with the rapid evolution of software and user demands. Some key pain points include:

1. **Time-Intensive Manual Testing**  
   Manual testing demands significant effort and time to cover potential scenarios and edge cases. As applications grow more complex, testing teams lag behind.

2. **Scalability Concerns**  
   Expanding QA to support larger projects or quicker product rollouts often results in resource strain, added costs, and potential bottlenecks.

3. **Testing Inconsistencies**  
   Human errors and subjectivity introduce inconsistencies. Different testers may yield varying results for identical test cases.

4. **Delayed Issue Detection**  
   Bugs uncovered late in development cycles inflate costs and harm user satisfaction.

5. **Incomplete Coverage for Sophisticated Systems**  
   Complex system dependencies and architectures lead to subpar testing coverage, risking potential product failures.
