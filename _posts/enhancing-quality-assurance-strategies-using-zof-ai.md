## The Limitations of Manual QA

Relying on manual QA processes can introduce inefficiencies and risks, including:

1. **Time Consumed by Manual Operations**
   Designing and performing tests manually is labor-intensive and slows down development cycles, especially in iterative environments.

2. **Human Errors can Lead to Oversights**
   Even skilled QA experts can miss bugs in the complex layers of today’s systems, resulting in errors that become expensive to fix later.

3. **Scaling Issues**
   Manual QA struggles to efficiently handle growing software complexity, such as testing large systems across platforms.

4. **Limited Test Coverage**
   Rapidly advancing codebases often leave edge cases or intricate issues untested.

5. **Subjectivity in Judgments**
   Manual testing can introduce variability due to the diverse perspectives of testers, leading to inconsistencies.
