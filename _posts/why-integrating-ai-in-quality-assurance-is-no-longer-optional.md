## Major Challenges in Traditional QA Practices

The inefficiencies of traditional QA methods become evident with the growing complexity and demand for rapid development cycles:

### 1. Challenges in Scalability
Testing software that handles millions of users across diverse devices and platforms becomes overwhelming with manual or outdated tools. 

### 2. Cost and Time Barriers
Manual QA practices are labor-intensive, leading to extended timelines and higher costs—delaying product launches and reducing business agility.

### 3. Increased Risk of Human Error
Repetitive tasks in manual testing expose teams to higher chances of mistakes, allowing bugs to slip through undetected.

### 4. Reactive, Not Proactive
Conventional QA identifies bugs too late, often during development phases or, worse, in production, resulting in expensive fixes and declining reliability.

### 5. No Predictive Analysis
Using just historical data, traditional QA fails to predict potential application performance issues, leaving users vulnerable to poor experiences.

Evidently, these limitations necessitate a more innovative approach, paving the way for AI-driven QA solutions.
